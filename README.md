##Content Summary##

#####Front#####
A TREATISE OF PATIENCE.WRITTEN BY FATHER FRANCIS ARIAS, OF THE Society of Ieſus, in his ſecond parte
1. THE EPISTLE DEDICATORY. To the right honorable and moſt noble Lady, the Lady B. A.

1. A TABLE OF THE CHAPTERS.

1. A prayer for obteining Patience of Almighty God.

#####Body#####

1. THE I. CHAPTER. In What the vertue of Patience conſiſts; and how the ſupernaturall fauours of God are neceſſary for our ſuffering the miſeries of this life with true Patience.

1. THE II. CHAPTER. Of the euils or incōueniences of payne which Chriſt our Lord beganne to ſuffer euen from his Conception, and Birth; and of the example of Patience, which he gaue vs therein.

1. THE III. CHAPTER. How Christ our Lord endured the company of the wicked, and of the example of Patience, which he gaue vs thereby.

1. THE IV. CHAPTER. Of the euils of paine, which Chriſt our Lord ſuffered in his Paſsion; and of the example of Patience, which he gaue vs, by ſuffering them with ſo great good will.

1. THE V. CHAPTER. Of the Patience, deſire, and loue, wherwith Chriſt our Lord indured all thoſe torments, for the example and edification of our ſoules.

1. THE VI. CHAPTER. Of the Patience wherewith wee ought to ſuffer the loſſe of temporall goods, and of the example which Chriſt our Lord gaue vs herein.

1. THE VII. CHAPTER. Of the Patience, Wherewith wee are to endure corporall infirmities; and of the example, which Chriſt our Lord hath giuen vs herein.

1. THE VIII. CHAPTER. Of other great benefits, which are drawen from corporall infirmities, when they are borne with Patience.

1. THE IX. CHAPTER. Wherein it is confirmed, by the example of Saints, how great the fruite of ſicknes, and other tribulation is; and how this is an effect of Gods mercy, which he she was towards his friends.

1. THE X. CHAPTER. Of the fruite of the encreaſe of Faith, and Patience, which wee muſt gather from the Patience, which the Saints haue shewed in ſuffering their infirmities and paines.

1. THE XI. CHAPTER. Of the Patience wherewith diſcomforts and ſpirituall deſolations, are to be endured; and of the example which Chriſt our Lord gaue hereof.

1. THE XII. CHAPTER. Of the neceſsity, and fruite of Patience; and how it is a ſigne of the elect and predestinated of God.

1. THE XIII. CHAPTER. How pleaſing the vertue of Patience is to God, and of the great fauours which he affoardes them, who ſuffer with Patience.

1. THE XIIII. CHAPTER. Of the meanes, whereby the vertue of Patience may be obtained; which is, that wee be watchfull to conſider the fruite thereof, and the examples whereby wee haue been taught it by Chriſt our Lord.

1. THE XV. CHAPTER. Of other meanes whereby wee are to obtaine, and conſerue the vertue of Patience, namely to conſider our ſinnes, and to reſort to Almighty God, for the remedy thereof.
